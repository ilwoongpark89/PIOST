About Us

Thermal Energy for Carbon Neutrality

PIOST Inc. is a deep-tech startup founded in 2024, dedicated to accelerating carbon neutrality through thermal energy storage and utilization. We develop technologies that store surplus renewable energy as high-temperature heat and convert it back to usable energy when needed.

The global transition to renewable energy faces a critical challenge: solar and wind power are intermittent, but energy demand is constant. PIOST addresses this gap by developing cost-effective thermal energy storage systems using abundant materials like rock and sand. Our Carnot Battery technology enables long-duration energy storage, providing a pathway to reliable, 24/7 clean energy and a carbon-neutral future.

The Challenge

Why Thermal Energy Must Be Stored

What is Thermal Energy Storage (TES)?

Thermal Energy Storage (TES) is a technology that stores energy in the form of heat for later use. Unlike batteries that store electricity chemically, TES systems capture and retain thermal energy in materials like rock, sand, or molten salt at high temperatures. When energy is needed, this stored heat can be released for heating applications or converted back to electricity through steam turbines.

Why is TES Essential for the Energy Transition?

The global shift to renewable energy faces a fundamental challenge: the sun doesn't always shine, and the wind doesn't always blow. Solar and wind power generation is inherently intermittent, creating a mismatch between when energy is produced and when it is consumed. Without effective storage solutions, excess renewable energy is wasted, and fossil fuel backup systems must fill the gaps.

TES offers a compelling solution to this challenge. Heat can be stored for extended periods with minimal loss, making TES ideal for long-duration energy storage measured in hours or even days. The storage materials—rock and sand—are abundant, inexpensive, and environmentally benign. Unlike lithium-ion batteries, TES systems don't rely on rare earth minerals or present fire hazards.

A Hopeful Future with TES

Imagine a world where every community can store summer's abundant solar energy to heat homes through winter. Where industrial waste heat is captured and repurposed rather than released into the atmosphere. Where remote villages gain energy independence through simple, durable storage systems that last for decades.

This future is within reach. TES technology has been proven at scale in concentrated solar power plants around the world. Now, advances in materials science and heat transfer engineering are making TES economically viable for a much broader range of applications—from residential heating to grid-scale energy storage.

The Opportunity

Why Carnot Battery?

What is a Carnot Battery?

A Carnot Battery is an advanced energy storage system that converts electricity to heat for storage, then converts that heat back to electricity when needed. Named after the French physicist Sadi Carnot who established the theoretical limits of heat engine efficiency, this technology represents the cutting edge of thermal energy storage.

The process works in three stages: First, excess electricity (typically from renewable sources) powers electric heaters to raise the temperature of a thermal storage medium to very high temperatures—in our case, exceeding 1,000°C. Second, this thermal energy is retained in insulated storage tanks with minimal loss. Third, when electricity is needed, the stored heat generates steam that drives a turbine to produce power.

Why Carnot Battery Over Other Storage Technologies?

While lithium-ion batteries dominate short-duration storage (2-4 hours), they become prohibitively expensive for longer durations. Pumped hydro storage offers long duration but requires specific geography. Carnot Batteries fill a critical gap: they provide cost-effective storage for 8-24+ hours using widely available materials and proven industrial components.

The economics are compelling. At high temperatures, thermal storage is 10-20 times cheaper per kWh than battery storage. The round-trip efficiency of modern Carnot Battery systems reaches 50-70%, competitive with other storage technologies when accounting for total system costs.

Powering the Grid of Tomorrow

Carnot Batteries represent more than an incremental improvement—they enable a fundamental transformation of our energy system. With cost-effective long-duration storage, renewable energy can provide reliable baseload power, replacing coal and natural gas plants that currently ensure grid stability.

The potential is enormous. The International Energy Agency estimates that achieving net-zero emissions by 2050 will require 2,000 GW of long-duration storage capacity globally. Carnot Batteries, built from steel, rock, and sand, can be manufactured at scale using existing industrial capabilities, creating jobs and economic opportunity while accelerating the clean energy transition.

At PIOST, we are working to make this vision a reality—one thermal storage system at a time.
